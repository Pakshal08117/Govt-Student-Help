## ğŸ›ï¸ Administrative Structure of India

### Understanding India's Governance Hierarchy

India follows a well-defined administrative structure that helps in effective governance and service delivery from the national level down to individual villages. This hierarchy ensures that government schemes and services reach every citizen across the country.

### Complete Administrative Hierarchy

The administrative structure of India is organized in **9 levels**, from the country level at the top to individual villages at the bottom:

| Level | Administrative Unit | Count | Status | Description |
|-------|-------------------|-------|--------|-------------|
| 1ï¸âƒ£ | **Country** | 1 | Fixed | India |
| 2ï¸âƒ£ | **States** | 28 | Fixed | States with their own governments |
| 3ï¸âƒ£ | **Union Territories** | 8 | Fixed | Centrally administered territories |
| 4ï¸âƒ£ | **States + UTs Combined** | 36 | Fixed | Total administrative regions |
| 5ï¸âƒ£ | **Districts** | ~806 | Approximate âš ï¸ | District-level divisions |
| 6ï¸âƒ£ | **Sub-districts** | ~7,800+ | Approximate âš ï¸ | Tehsil/Taluka/Mandal/Sub-division |
| 7ï¸âƒ£ | **Development Blocks** | Several thousand | Approximate âš ï¸ | Block-level units (state-dependent) |
| 8ï¸âƒ£ | **Gram Panchayats** | ~2.5 lakh+ | Approximate âš ï¸ | Village-level councils (~250,000+) |
| 9ï¸âƒ£ | **Villages** | ~6 lakh+ | Approximate âš ï¸ | Rural settlements (~600,000+) |

### Understanding Sub-district Terminology

**Important Note**: At Level 6 (Sub-districts), different states use different names for the **same administrative level**. These are NOT different levels - they are just different names used in different regions:

| Term | States/UTs Using This Term | Example |
|------|---------------------------|---------|
| **Tehsil** | Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, Haryana, Punjab, Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Jammu & Kashmir, Delhi, Chandigarh, Ladakh, and others | Lucknow has multiple Tehsils |
| **Taluka** | Maharashtra, Gujarat, Karnataka, Goa, Dadra and Nagar Haveli and Daman and Diu | Pune has multiple Talukas |
| **Mandal** | Andhra Pradesh, Telangana | Hyderabad has multiple Mandals |
| **Sub-division** | West Bengal, Assam, Bihar, Jharkhand, Odisha | Kolkata has multiple Sub-divisions |

**Example**: If you live in Maharashtra, your address includes a "Taluka". If you live in Uttar Pradesh, it includes a "Tehsil". Both serve the same administrative function!

âš ï¸ **Important Information for Citizens**:

Some administrative counts are marked as "approximate" because:

1. **Districts**: The number of districts changes when:

3. **Blocks, Panchayats, Villages**: These numbers change due to:
